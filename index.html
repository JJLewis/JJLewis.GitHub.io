Timeline

My journey so far.

  • 2011-2012

    A Spark of Interest

    I want to make something. I love computers. Apps are cool. Let's write an app. With help from Youtube, online tutorials and two For Dummies books I never read, I set off on the great journey of becoming a software developer.

  • 4 August 2012

    First App Released

    Originally a mess of code and storyboard linking, now a clean efficient app. I wrote this as one of the first things I learnt was a random number generator function. I wanted to make something with the little that I knew.

  • 8 October 2012

    Simple Interest Calculator

    Having just learnt about Simple Interest in Math class, I decided to take my knew knowlege and bundle it up into an app and release it. This took about 3 days, since I was still new to this stuff.

  • 10 Janurary 2013

    Dodgeball

    I watched a Youtube video on making a simple dodge a ball game with one ball, I extended on it by adding power ups and more balls. It had many bugs and looked ugly. It has since been updated and built on SpriteKit which has relativly better looking animations and explosions, while also maintaining 60FPS.

  • 23 September 2013

    Crypto

    I had the idea for this app in Year 7, but only felt comfortable doing it in Year 9. It simply replaces characters in a string you type in, to give you an 'encrypted' version, either a Binary, Hex, Leet(L337), or a custom scheme. You can email, tweet, post, and copy the output to with it as you wish. This also works backwords in decrypting.

  • 30 September 2014

    Tip Calculator

    I was bored on a weekend so put this together. I don't think anybody needs this, since it is simple percentage math.

  • 13 November 2014

    Compound Interest Calculator

    After having learnt the mathematical way to calculate this, I decided to bundle this up in the form of an app and add a graph function so that anyone and everyone can understand and make use of the eighth wonder of the world.
    "Compound interest is the eighth wonder of the world. He who understands it, earns it... He doesn't... pays it." ~ Albert Einstein

A Little Bit About Me

My interest in computers and programming began when I was in Year 7 (2011). I wanted to create something, and at the time, computer and software interested me. I started watching simple Youtube tutorials to learn. From the most basic Hello World iPhone app to a simple calculator app. I think that the only reason that I focus on iOS now is that I found the iPhone Simulator bundled with Xcode cool. From the completion of my first app, my interest snowballed and I began programming in my free time after school. I have started and finished many projects, but there have been some that my skill was not high enough to complete. I got my Apple Developer account under my mum's name in Year 8. Since I taught myself how to code, I found later on that I left out many rather quite important things, such as Arrays and Dictionaries... I went back and updated all my apps to be more efficient after I discovered those data types. After I felt comfortable enough in Objective-C (my first language), I played with other languages, in particular, Python and Swift when it came out. Overtime, my way of thinking and programming has changed and gotten much more efficient and readable. I have two large projects that I am very proud of, both of which should be released in the near future.

Decider

My first ever public app.

Decider was the first iOS app that I ever wrote. Back when I was in Year 8, I wrote this app. Back then, my knowledge of algorithms was super close to zero. The only control structures/language functions I new at the time was IF statements, how to assign a text value to a UITextfield, and how to generate random numbers into an int variable. Other than that, all of the GUI was done in a Storyboard file and individual classes where created to match each ViewController. There were literally 1000s of lines of duplicate code and meaningless rubbish at the time. I wish I still had the project file, it’s gone somewhere now, but I’ll be sure to post it when I do find it. It will likely make quite a few developers out there keel over and cry.

The good news is, though, that I completely rewrote the app in Swift, funnily enough, as a way to learn Swift. This time though, I knew how to handle loops, arrays, dictionaries, and other programming language features. At the moment, I fear opening the Swift project because since the Swift syntax has changed since the last time I had opened it. I fear being smacked in the face with tens or even hundreds of little red triangles that tell me XCode can’t compile the project due to syntax issues.

When I first released Decider, all I wanted was about 100 people to pay $0.99 for the app so I could make my membership fees back. Unfortunately things didn’t go so well… So I added banner ads everywhere and made the app free.

Try it out! It should be quite ok/usable now.

Download it from the AppStore for Free.
